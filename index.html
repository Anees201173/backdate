Inn today’s world the power of data science is widely acknowledged, even in resource-rich settings and advanced countries.The prevailing consensus appreciates the power and potential of analytics and data science innovations. Imagine a starkly different situation in a country like Nigeria where the setting is resource-scarce and having access to healthcare is a challenge. Here, marginalised and highly vulnerable populations face even greater difficulties.

One of the most powerful benefits of data science is its ability to save lives and transform healthcare access, particularly for underserved communities disproportionately impacted by HIV/AIDS. This is especially true for marginalized key populations like LGBTQ+, female sex workers, and people who inject drugs. Few years ago when Data science was a budding industry, it became a veritable tool used to improve antiretroviral treatment adherence and outcomes.

Then, I had the opportunity to tap into data science’s potential by working as a volunteer on a PEPFAR-funded program through the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and implemented through Caritas Nigeria’s sponsored Community-Based Organization, Sisters Against HIV and Cancer Initiative (SACHI). This project involved building a data science model that could predict patient behavior and optimize HIV/AIDS treatment plans.

Primary Objective

Pioneering the use of analytics solutions among marginalized key populations living with HIV/AIDS in Delta State, Nigeria. The project aimed to develop a predictive model to ensure enhanced routine monitoring of patient adherence to Antiretroviral Therapy (ART) among key populations living with HIV/AIDS. By predicting patient behavior and treatment responses, the model aimed to optimize treatment management and outcomes, for the marginalized key populations like LGBTQ+, female sex workers, and people who inject drugs, in Delta State, Nigeria.

Technologies Used

• Python (used for data preprocessing, analysis, and modeling).

• Machine Learning Algorithms (used to find the most accurate predictive model while Random forests were effective).

• Data visualization tools (used Matplotlib and Seaborn to identify critical insights).

ART Intake Prediction

The random forest model was found effective with 93% overall accuracy. The random forest classification report generated each trained model’s precision, recall, F1 score, and support proving its effectiveness. These outcomes can be viewed here. The following were the classes for which the model was trained for ART Intake prediction:

• Class 0 – Majority Class (Active)

• Class 1 – LTFU

• Class 2 – Discontinued Care

• Class 3 – Transferred Out

• Class 4 – Death

Some other features incorporated in the model include appointment status, biometric status, sex, current weight date, and more, all together 14 in number.

However, by analyzing the patient reactions and identifying influential factors, this model provides insights into how patients respond to ART treatment. The following were the highlights.

• There were variables with over 50% null values, which were eliminated to ensure model accuracy.

• The analysis helped us understand the factors that affected ART adherence, patterns and correlations, patient behavior, and responses to treatment.

• The model provided high precision and recall for the Majority Class (Active) but had some challenges predicting less common outcomes such as Discontinued Care and Transferred Out.

• Identifying the key features that affect patient adherence helped provide actionable insights for healthcare providers and streamline personalized treatment plans.

Impact and Recognition

The model increased adherence rates by 27%, potentially leading to improved health outcomes for thousands of individuals, particularly among these marginalized key populations.The work was a textbook case of how data science can be harnessed to provide real life solutions with tangible impact.

Our efforts were appreciated by various experts in the field of both data science and public health, including the following:

• Charles Ogbonna, a seasoned health informatics and data expert currently serving as the Technical Advisor for Health Informatics at the US Military HIV Research Program, recognized the project’s significant impact on the program.

• Dr. Courage Ekejiuba, a public health expert, and Victor Adamu, a public health expert on key populations with the CDC, also acknowledged the project’s contribution to improving HIV/AIDS treatment adherence.

The project’s success and impact, a direct testament to the power of data science, were pivotal in helping me and my team gaining recognition. Our efforts were rewarded by the CDC for outstanding contributions.

This project served as an ideal showcase of expertise and knowledge of analytics, especially in a period when its application was more groundbreaking. Additionally, it demonstrates the ability to work effectively across disciplines with colleagues, narrowing the gap between data science and real world impact.Although building a model was an integral component of the project it is important to understand that it’s true values lies in the ability to harness the potential of data science to create a real-world solution with tangible impact.
